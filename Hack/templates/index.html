By Hong Yi, Hubert, Michelle, and Jovian

Overview

Our solution ultilises 2D AI Geospatial Mapping and Simulation to carry out Predictive Analysis of Natural Disasters' Impact on Endangered Species in Biodiverse Ecosystems.

How is the Comparison of Conservation Status across Different Animal Class?

The bar charts show how many species fall into different conservation status which include endangered, critically endangered, least concern, vulnerable and near threatened across four different animal classes, aves, amphibias, reptiles, and mammals. From the graph, we can see that amphibias have the highest counts of endangered and critically endangered species followed by reptiles. Aves also show significant number of threatened species even thought not as many as amphibia and reptiles. Mammals have the lowest counts of endangered and critically endangered species. Overall, this suggests that amphibias and reptiles face greater conservation challenges as many of the species fall under threatened or critically threatened.

The donut chart provides a better visualisation for the distribution of the conservation status to see which classes are generally highly at risk and endangered. We can see that mammalia has the most endangered and critically endangered species compared to other class. This means that mammalia needs more attention to prevent them from getting extinct.


Which natural disasters impact each species of animals most?

From the donut chart, we can see that hurricanes impact the most out of all the natural disasters with more than 60% of the disaster occurances across all species. Birds rarely get affected by volcanoes and earthquakes with 2.7% and 3.1% frequency respectively. The remaining species tend to get affected more by volcanoes (Average of 14.9%) compared to earthquakes (Average of 11.2%).


What is the relationship between natural disaster impact and conservation status?

From the box plots, it can be seen that earthquakes generally have middle range and similar average impacts across all conservation status. On the other hand, volcanoes and hurricanes tend to show higher average impacts especially for endangered and critically endangered groups with wider range and interquartile range which suggest that species are already facing high risk to these type of disasters. Overall, the data suggests that species that are at endangered and critically endangered are mainly sufferred from volcano and hurricane.


What are the risk levels different disasters have on different animal species?

We can observe that Reptiles are at the highest risk during volcano eruptions and hurricanes at 54.31% and 53.97% high-risk while amphibians are the most prone to earthquakes at 65.38% high-risk. Mammals are the overall least prone to such natural disasters with an average 40.43% high-risk. Hurricanes have an average high risk to all species types compared to earthquakes and volcanoes at 48.94% high-risk. Earthquakes poses the highest risk to reptiles, amphibians and birds.
